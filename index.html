HOME SWEET HOME

I was raised in Henderson, NV, a beautiful city right next to Las Vegas. This meant vacations were either to California to Disneyland and the beach or to the mountains of Utah to visit snow. Having been born in '92, I've had the pleasures of experiencing the early technologies of the 90s and then welcoming the age of technology in the 2000s.

BOLIVIA

I took 2 years out of my life to be an LDS missionary and serve the people of Bolivia. There I had to learn Spanish and even Quechua to get by. Adapting to the Bolivian culture - food, living conditions, and social environment was difficult, but well worth it. They have become my family and will always have a place in my heart.

COLLEGE YEARS

One day while studying at BYU, Mark Zuckerberg, whom I idolized at the time, came to speak to us students. He mentioned that he double majored in Computer Science and Psychology. I decided then to take on this interesting combo and major in Psychology to find out why humans behave the way they do. I also minored in Management.

PURSING MY PASSION

I worked IT for a year and a half managing the websites computer hardware. I became infatuated with the "magic" of code and realized that I can learn anything. I began teaching myself HTML/CSS and Java from online schools. My last semester at BYU, my entrepreneurship professor recommended I learn code full-time at DevMoutain.

WHAT'S NEXT

I finished DevMountain's immersive Javascript bootcamp in Provo, UT in June 2016 and got hired back on as a mentor and web developer there. I was happy to give back to the school that taught me so much and am excited and optimistic for future endevours in computer programming.
